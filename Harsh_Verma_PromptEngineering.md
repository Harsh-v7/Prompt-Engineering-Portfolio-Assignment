## Part 1: Prompt Design & Iteration

### 1.1 Scenario A: Technical Explanation

**Goal:** Explain "neural networks" to a 10-year-old.

**Initial Prompt (V1):**
> Explain the concept of neural networks to a 10 year old child.

**V1 Output:**
> Neural networks are a way for computers to learn, kind of like how people learn from experience. Imagine you're teaching a friend how to recognize a cat. At first, they might not know what makes a cat different from a dog. But after seeing lots of pictures, they start noticing things like pointy ears, whiskers, and a long tail. A neural network works in a similar way. It is made up of many tiny parts called "neurons" that work together like a team. Each neuron looks for small clues. One might look for ears, another for eyes, and another for fur. When they combine their clues, they can decide whether the picture is a cat or a dog.


**Refined Prompt (V2):**
> Explain neural networks in very simple language for a 10-year-old child. Use examples from daily life.

**V2 Output:**
> Imagine you are learning to tell the difference between apples and oranges. At first, you might get confused, but after seeing many examples, you start noticing patterns such as color, shape, and texture. A neural network learns in the same way. It is made up of many small parts that work together like a team of helpers. If the computer is looking at a picture of a dog, some helpers might look at the ears, others at the eyes, and others at the tail. They combine what they find and decide whether the picture is a dog. The more examples the neural network sees, the better it becomes at making correct decisions. Neural networks are used in everyday technologies such as face recognition on phones, voice assistants, video recommendations, and translation apps. In simple terms, a neural network is like a computer brain that learns from experience and improves with practice.
